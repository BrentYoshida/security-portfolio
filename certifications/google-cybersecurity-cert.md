# Google Cybersecurity Certificate
**Issuer:** Google / Coursera  
**Completed:** April 2026  
**Duration:** ~3 weeks  
**Context:** Completed while working part-time nights in hospitality and simultaneously building this security portfolio  

---

## Overview

The Google Cybersecurity Certificate on Coursera is an 8-course program covering security fundamentals, network security, Linux, Python, SQL, and incident detection and response. It's designed as an entry point into the field; and it delivers on that. By the end, you've touched enough ground to know what you don't know yet, which is exactly what a foundation cert should do.

---

## What the Course Does Well

The content is genuinely engaging. Google built this with multiple learning styles in mind: video lectures, hands-on labs, readings, and interviews with working Google security professionals talking about their actual careers and how they got there. For someone coming into security from a non-technical background, those practitioner interviews carry real weight. This isn't abstract theory being handed down from a textbook, it's people explaining how they use this knowledge at work every day.

The pacing is deliberate without being slow. Topics build on each other in a way that makes the curriculum feel designed rather than assembled.

---

## Bash: The Moment Everything Clicked

The Linux and Bash modules were the highlight of the entire course. Typing into a command line for the first time was something I'd wanted to do for a long time; there's something about having direct control over a system through text that felt immediately right. Learning that a few commands could navigate a filesystem, find files, filter output, and chain operations together was one of those moments where a door opens and you realize how much is behind it.

That rabbit hole went deep. After the Bash modules I kept going, spending hours learning commands beyond what the course covered, testing combinations, breaking things and figuring out why. That curiosity is what eventually led me to OverTheWire Bandit and building out a documented write-up series. The Google cert opened the door. Bash pulled me through it.

---

## Python — The Honest Struggle

Python came up shortly after Bash and was genuinely difficult: not because the language is hard, but because my brain was already wired into Bash thinking. Every time I started a Python script my instinct was to reach for Bash syntax. The two languages handle logic differently enough that the context switch took real effort.

The way through it: YouTube videos during commutes to and from work. Found a few Python learning games that helped specific syntax patterns stick; particularly the colon at the end of function and loop declarations, which kept tripping me up. Repetition through different formats eventually made it click.

The struggle with Python taught something more useful than Python syntax: how to identify when a learning approach isn't working and change it mid-stream. Supplementing the course content with outside resources rather than grinding the same material that wasn't landing; that's a study skill that's paid off consistently since.

---

## What This Cert Is and Isn't

The Google cert is a foundation, not a credential that opens technical doors on its own. It won't get you a penetration testing interview. What it does is build the vocabulary and conceptual framework that makes everything that comes after it faster to learn. Security+, the ISC2 CC, TryHackMe, HackTheBox; all of it lands better because the Google cert established the baseline first.

It's also the most accessible entry point into the field for someone without a technical background. The course doesn't assume you know anything going in. That's both its limitation and its value.

## Real Conditions

Three weeks. Working part-time nights in hospitality on an irregular schedule. Early mornings before shifts, late nights after closing, commute time for audio content. Not ideal study conditions by any measure: which made finishing it feel like a legitimate indicator of what's possible when the interest is genuine.

Cybersecurity had been on my radar for a long time. Once I started, the hours weren't something I tracked. They were just what happened when you're engaged in something your brain actually wants to do.

## What It Led To

Completing the Google cert built enough momentum to move directly into the ISC2 CC domains. Finishing all five within 48 hours. It also confirmed that the interest wasn't theoretical. The command line wasn't intimidating. The concepts weren't opaque. The field was learnable and the learning was genuinely enjoyable.

That was the most important thing the course delivered: confirmation.
